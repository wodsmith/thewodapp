# WODsmith User Personas

This document outlines detailed user personas for all types of users who interact with the WODsmith platform. These personas follow industry standards and include demographics, goals, pain points, motivations, and user journeys.

---

## Competition Users

### Persona 1: First-Time Competition Judge - "Jessica the Newbie Judge"

**Demographics:**
- Age: 28
- Location: Austin, TX
- Occupation: Marketing Manager & CrossFit Enthusiast
- Tech Savviness: Medium (comfortable with apps, needs intuitive interfaces)
- CrossFit Experience: 2 years training, never judged before

**Context:**
Jessica was invited by a friend who owns a local box to judge at a community throwdown. She's excited but nervous about doing it right.

**Goals:**
- Understand scoring requirements quickly
- Not embarrass herself or make mistakes
- Access athlete information and standards easily
- Submit scores confidently and correctly
- Learn the platform with minimal instruction

**Pain Points:**
- Has never used WODsmith before
- Limited time to learn (day-of event)
- Worried about technical difficulties during competition
- Doesn't know competition terminology or workflows
- Fear of holding up the competition with user errors

**Motivations:**
- Help her CrossFit community
- Gain experience in the competition scene
- Potentially judge more events in the future
- Do a good job and be asked back

**User Journey:**
1. Receives judging invitation via email/text
2. First login on mobile device at competition venue
3. Needs immediate orientation and guidance
4. Judges 3-5 heats throughout the day
5. May never use the platform again, or becomes occasional user

**Technology Comfort:**
- Uses iPhone daily
- Comfortable with Instagram, email, basic apps
- Needs clear visual cues and confirmation messages
- Prefers mobile-first experience

**Quote:**
> "I just want to make sure I'm doing this right and not slowing everyone down. Is there a quick tutorial or something?"

Patricia manages billing, subscriptions, and finances for a 3-location CrossFit franchise. She's evaluating WODsmith's billing system and integration with their accounting tools.

**Goals:**
- Understand exact pricing and billing structure
- Integrate with accounting software (QuickBooks)
- Manage subscriptions across multiple locations
- Track expenses and ROI per location
- Ensure compliance and proper invoicing
- Consolidated billing and payment

**Pain Points:**
- Surprise fees or unclear pricing
- Manual data entry into accounting system
- Difficult to track costs across multiple locations
- Payment failures and billing issues
- Poor invoicing and receipt management
- Can't forecast costs accurately

**Motivations:**
- Accurate financial tracking and reporting
- Cost control and predictability
- Efficient month-end close process
- Vendor management and consolidation
- Support finance team's needs

**User Journey:**
1. Reviews pricing page and billing FAQs
2. Requests detailed quote for multiple locations
3. Asks about volume discounts and contracts
4. Tests billing integration with QuickBooks
5. Reviews invoices and payment process
6. Evaluates total cost of ownership

**Technology Comfort:**
- Expert with accounting software
- Expects clear billing and invoicing
- Needs export capabilities (CSV, API)
- Values automated reconciliation

**Quote:**
> "I need transparent, predictable pricing and clean integration with our accounting system. No surprise fees, and I need to see costs per location."

## Notes

These personas should be:
- **Reviewed quarterly** - User needs evolve
- **Referenced in design decisions** - "Which persona does this serve?"
- **Used in user testing** - Recruit participants matching persona profiles
- **Shared across team** - Product, Engineering, Marketing, Support
- **Updated based on data** - Analytics, support tickets, user interviews

For each new feature or change, ask:
1. Which persona(s) does this serve?
2. Does it solve a key pain point or support a primary goal?
3. Is the experience appropriate for their tech comfort level?
4. Does it fit their typical user journey and device usage?
